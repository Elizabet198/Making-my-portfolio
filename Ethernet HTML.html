For my portfolio, I created an Ethernet crossover cable by manually assembling and crimping both ends using RJ45 connectors. I began by stripping the outer jacket of a twisted pair Ethernet cable to expose the eight internal wires, then untwisted and arranged them according to the T568A standard on one end and the T568B standard on the other. This specific wiring configuration is known as a crossover cable, which is traditionally used to connect two devices directly, such as two computers, without the need for a network switch. After aligning the wires in the correct order, I inserted them into RJ45 connectors and used a crimping tool to secure the pins, completing a functional, hand-made Ethernet cable. This process strengthened my understanding of networking hardware and the practical application of Ethernet wiring standards.
